# Jack Dorsey

## Introduction

Jack Dorsey is an American technology entrepreneur and philanthropist, best known as the co-founder and former CEO of Twitter, a social media platform that revolutionized online communication, and as the co-founder and CEO of Block, Inc. (formerly Square, Inc.), a financial services and digital payments company. Born on November 19, 1976, in St. Louis, Missouri, Dorsey has played a significant role in shaping modern internet culture and financial technology. His work has focused on creating platforms and tools that facilitate communication and commerce, earning him recognition as a prominent figure in the tech industry [Biography.com](https://www.biography.com/business-leaders/jack-dorsey).

## Early Life and Historical Background

Jack Patrick Dorsey grew up in St. Louis, Missouri, where he developed an early interest in computers and programming. As a teenager, he became fascinated with the idea of mapping and logistics, inspired by listening to police radio scanners and imagining how to track movement in real-time. At age 14, he wrote software for dispatch routing, some of which is still used by taxi companies today [Forbes Profile](https://www.forbes.com/profile/jack-dorsey/).

Dorsey attended Missouri University of Science and Technology before transferring to New York University (NYU), though he dropped out before completing his degree to pursue entrepreneurial ventures. In 2000, he moved to Oakland, California, where he started a company to dispatch couriers, taxis, and emergency services via the web. This early experience with real-time coordination would later influence the creation of Twitter [The New York Times](https://www.nytimes.com/2011/03/25/technology/internet/25twitter.html).

In 2006, Dorsey, along with Biz Stone and Evan Williams, co-founded Twitter, initially as a side project while working at a podcasting company called Odeo. The idea stemmed from Dorsey’s vision of a platform for short, real-time status updates. Twitter launched publicly in July 2006 and quickly gained traction as a tool for communication and information sharing. Dorsey served as Twitter’s first CEO from 2007 to 2008, before stepping down amid internal conflicts. He returned to the role in 2015 and led the company until 2021 [CNN Business](https://www.cnn.com/2021/11/29/tech/jack-dorsey-twitter-ceo-steps-down/index.html).

Parallel to his work with Twitter, Dorsey co-founded Square, Inc. in 2009 with Jim McKelvey. Square aimed to simplify credit card payments for small businesses through a mobile card reader. The company expanded into other financial services, including peer-to-peer payments via Cash App, and rebranded as Block, Inc. in 2021 to reflect its broader focus on blockchain and cryptocurrency technologies [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2021-12-01/square-changes-corporate-name-to-block-in-nod-to-crypto-ambitions).

## Current Status and Relevance

As of 2023, Jack Dorsey remains a central figure in the tech and financial sectors. After stepping down as Twitter’s CEO in November 2021, he focused on his role as CEO of Block, Inc., where he continues to drive innovation in digital payments and cryptocurrency adoption. Under his leadership, Block has invested heavily in Bitcoin and blockchain technology, reflecting Dorsey’s belief in decentralized financial systems as the future of money [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/technology/twitter-ceo-jack-dorsey-steps-down-2021-11-29/).

Dorsey’s influence extends beyond business. He has been an advocate for internet freedom and privacy, often speaking out on issues like censorship and data security. His decision to step away from Twitter (now rebranded as X under Elon Musk’s leadership) was partly motivated by a desire to allow the platform to evolve independently, though he has occasionally commented on its direction post-2021 [The Verge](https://www.theverge.com/2022/4/26/23043558/jack-dorsey-twitter-elon-musk-takeover-comments).

Additionally, Dorsey is involved in philanthropy through initiatives like #startsmall, a fund he established in 2020 with a $1 billion commitment from his personal wealth to support COVID-19 relief, universal basic income experiments, and other causes. His transparent approach to philanthropy, including public tracking of donations, has garnered attention [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2020/04/07/twitter-ceo-jack-dorsey-pledges-1-billion-of-square-equity-to-fight-coronavirus.html).

## Notable Facts and Details

- **Twitter’s Origin**: The idea for Twitter came from Dorsey’s fascination with short, SMS-like messages. The platform’s original name was “twttr,” inspired by the five-character limit of SMS codes and the sound of a bird chirp [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2011/03/22/the-real-history-of-twitter/).
- **Leadership Style**: Dorsey is known for a minimalist and disciplined lifestyle, including practices like intermittent fasting and meditation. He has cited these habits as key to maintaining focus while managing two major companies simultaneously [Business Insider](https://www.businessinsider.com/jack-dorsey-daily-routine-twitter-square-2019-4).
- **Net Worth**: As of 2023, Dorsey’s net worth is estimated at over $4 billion, largely tied to his stakes in Block, Inc. and Twitter/X [Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/profile/jack-dorsey/).
- **Bitcoin Advocacy**: Dorsey is a vocal proponent of Bitcoin, describing it as a tool for economic empowerment. Block, Inc. has integrated Bitcoin transactions into its platforms, and Dorsey has personally invested in Bitcoin mining initiatives [CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/business/2021/10/15/jack-dorsey-says-square-is-building-a-bitcoin-mining-system/).
- **Controversies**: Dorsey faced criticism during his tenure at Twitter for the platform’s handling of misinformation, censorship, and political bias. His decisions, such as the permanent suspension of former U.S. President Donald Trump’s account in 2021, sparked debates over free speech [BBC News](https://www.bbc.com/news/technology-55578403).
