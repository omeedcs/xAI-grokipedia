# Peru

## Introduction

Peru, officially known as the Republic of Peru, is a country in western South America, bordered by Ecuador and Colombia to the north, Brazil to the east, Bolivia to the southeast, Chile to the south, and the Pacific Ocean to the west. Covering an area of approximately 1.28 million square kilometers, Peru is the 19th largest country in the world and home to a population of about 33 million people as of 2023. Its capital, Lima, serves as the political, cultural, and economic hub of the nation. Peru is renowned for its rich history, diverse geography, and vibrant cultural heritage, which includes the ancient Inca civilization and a blend of indigenous, European, and African influences [Peru Overview - World Bank](https://www.worldbank.org/en/country/peru/overview).

Peru’s landscape is divided into three main geographical regions: the coastal desert, the Andean highlands, and the Amazon rainforest, each contributing to the country’s biodiversity and cultural diversity. As a developing nation, Peru faces challenges such as economic inequality and political instability but remains a significant player in Latin America due to its natural resources and historical importance.

## Historical Background

### Pre-Columbian Era
Peru’s history stretches back thousands of years, with evidence of human habitation dating to around 9000 BCE. The region was home to several advanced pre-Columbian civilizations, including the Norte Chico civilization (one of the world’s oldest, dating to 3200 BCE) and the Chavín culture. However, the most famous of these is the Inca Empire, which emerged in the 13th century and, at its peak in the early 16th century, spanned much of western South America. The Incas, with their capital at Cusco, developed sophisticated agricultural techniques, road systems, and architectural wonders like Machu Picchu [Inca Empire - Britannica](https://www.britannica.com/topic/Inca).

### Colonial Period
In 1532, Spanish conquistador Francisco Pizarro arrived in Peru, leading to the fall of the Inca Empire after the capture and execution of Emperor Atahualpa. Peru became a key part of the Spanish colonial empire, with Lima established as the capital of the Viceroyalty of Peru in 1542. The colonial era was marked by the exploitation of indigenous labor in silver mines, such as those in Potosí (now in Bolivia), and the imposition of Spanish culture and Catholicism. This period also saw significant demographic changes due to disease and intermarriage, creating a mestizo population [Colonial Peru - History.com](https://www.history.com/topics/south-america/peru).

### Independence and Modern History
Peru declared independence from Spain on July 28, 1821, under the leadership of José de San Martín, though full independence was achieved only after battles led by Simón Bolívar in 1824. The 19th and early 20th centuries were marked by political instability, economic struggles, and territorial losses, including during the War of the Pacific (1879–1883) against Chile. In the 20th century, Peru experienced military dictatorships, economic reforms, and internal conflict, notably the Shining Path insurgency in the 1980s and 1990s, a violent Maoist guerrilla movement that caused thousands of deaths [Shining Path - BBC](https://www.bbc.com/news/world-latin-america-15656740). Since the early 2000s, Peru has seen relative political stability and economic growth, though challenges persist.

## Current Status and Relevance

As of 2023, Peru is a presidential representative democratic republic with a multi-party system. The current president, Dina Boluarte, assumed office in December 2022 following a period of political turmoil that included the impeachment of her predecessor, Pedro Castillo. Peru’s economy is classified as upper-middle-income by the World Bank, driven by mining (copper, gold, silver), agriculture, and tourism. The country is one of the world’s leading producers of copper and has significant reserves of other minerals [Peru Economy - CIA World Factbook](https://www.cia.gov/the-world-factbook/countries/peru/#economy).

Peru plays a key role in regional and global affairs as a member of organizations like the United Nations, the Organization of American States (OAS), and the Pacific Alliance. However, it faces ongoing challenges, including income inequality, corruption, and environmental issues related to mining and deforestation in the Amazon. Social unrest, often tied to indigenous rights and resource extraction, remains a point of tension [Peru Protests - Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/americas/peru-protests-continue-despite-new-presidents-pledge-dialogue-2023-01-20/).

## Notable Facts and Details

- **Geography and Biodiversity**: Peru’s diverse terrain includes the Andes Mountains, with peaks like Huascarán (6,768 meters), the Amazon Basin (covering 60% of the country), and a 2,414-kilometer coastline. It is one of the world’s 17 megadiverse countries, hosting numerous unique species, such as the jaguar and the Andean condor [Peru Biodiversity - WWF](https://www.worldwildlife.org/places/peru).
- **Cultural Heritage**: Peru is home to 12 UNESCO World Heritage Sites, including Machu Picchu, often called one of the New Seven Wonders of the World. Its culture blends indigenous traditions with Spanish, African, and Asian influences, evident in its cuisine (e.g., ceviche), music, and festivals like Inti Raymi [Machu Picchu - UNESCO](https://whc.unesco.org/en/list/274/).
- **Languages**: While Spanish is the official language, spoken by about 84% of the population, indigenous languages like Quechua (13%) and Aymara (2%) are also widely used, reflecting Peru’s multicultural identity [Languages of Peru - Ethnologue](https://www.ethnologue.com/country/PE).
- **Economy and Resources**: Peru’s mining sector accounts for over 60% of its exports, with copper alone making up a significant portion. Agriculture, including quinoa and potatoes (both native to Peru), and tourism also contribute to the economy.
- **Social Issues**: Despite economic growth, over 20% of Peruvians live below the poverty line, with rural and indigenous communities disproportionately affected. Access to education and healthcare remains uneven [Poverty in Peru - World Bank](https://www.worldbank.org/en/country/peru/overview#3).

## Related Topics

- **Inca Empire**: The historical civilization that dominated much of Peru before Spanish colonization, known for its architectural and cultural achievements.
- **Machu Picchu**: An iconic 15th-century Inca citadel and a major tourist destination in Peru.
- **Amazon Rainforest**: A significant portion of Peru’s territory is part of this vital global ecosystem, facing threats from deforestation.
- **Lima**: The capital city, a cultural and economic center with a rich colonial history.
- **Andean Culture**: The traditions and lifestyles of indigenous peoples in Peru’s highlands, including Quechua and Aymara communities.
